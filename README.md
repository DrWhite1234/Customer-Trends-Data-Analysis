# 🛍️ Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

An end-to-end data analytics project that turns raw retail transaction data into business insights — data cleaning and feature engineering in **Python**, business-question analysis in **SQL**, and an interactive **Power BI** dashboard.

## 📌 Business Problem

A retail company wants to understand shifting customer purchasing patterns across demographics, product categories, and sales channels, and to identify which factors — discounts, reviews, seasonality, or payment preferences — drive purchase decisions and repeat business.

**Overarching question:** *How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?*

## 🗄️ Dataset

`customer_shopping_behavior.csv` — 3,900 customer transaction records across 18 original fields, covering:

- **Demographics:** Age, Gender, Location (50 U.S. states)
- **Purchase details:** Item Purchased, Category, Purchase Amount (USD), Size, Color, Season
- **Engagement signals:** Review Rating, Subscription Status, Shipping Type, Discount Applied, Promo Code Used, Previous Purchases, Payment Method, Frequency of Purchases

*(Add a link here to the original source, e.g. Kaggle, if this dataset came from somewhere public.)*

**Engineered features (added during Python cleaning):**
- `age_group` — customers bucketed into quartiles: Young Adult, Adult, Middle-aged, Senior
- `purchase_frequency_days` — converts each customer's purchase-frequency label (e.g. "Weekly", "Fortnightly") into an approximate number of days between purchases

## 🧰 Tech Stack & Workflow

1. **Python (pandas)** — `Customer_Shopping_Behavior_Analysis.ipynb`
   - Imported and profiled the raw dataset (`.info()`, `.describe()`, null checks)
   - Imputed missing `Review Rating` values with the median rating per product category
   - Standardized column names to snake_case
   - Engineered `age_group` and `purchase_frequency_days`
   - Dropped `promo_code_used` after confirming it was perfectly redundant with `discount_applied`
   - Loaded the cleaned data into a SQL database (PostgreSQL / MySQL / SQL Server, via SQLAlchemy)

2. **SQL** — `customer_behavior_sql_queries.sql`
   - 10 business-question queries covering revenue by gender, discount behavior, product ratings, shipping, subscriptions, customer segmentation, and age-group revenue contribution

3. **Power BI** — `customer_behavior_dashboard.pbix`
   - Interactive dashboard visualizing the customer segments, revenue trends, and purchase drivers surfaced in the SQL analysis

## ❓ Business Questions Answered (SQL)

1. Total revenue generated by male vs. female customers
2. Customers who used a discount but still spent above the average purchase amount
3. Top 5 products by average review rating
4. Average purchase amount: Standard vs. Express shipping
5. Average spend and total revenue: subscribers vs. non-subscribers
6. Top 5 products by percentage of purchases with discounts applied
7. Customer segmentation (New / Returning / Loyal) by previous purchase count
8. Top 3 most-purchased products within each category
9. Whether repeat buyers (5+ previous purchases) are more likely to subscribe
10. Revenue contribution by age group

## 📈 Key Insights

- **The customer base skews heavily "Loyal":** ~80% of customers already have more than 10 previous purchases, while only ~2% are first-time buyers — repeat purchasing is already strong, so growth likely depends more on new customer acquisition than on retention alone.
- **Subscription doesn't move average spend:** subscribers spend $59.49 on average vs. $59.87 for non-subscribers — almost no difference. Non-subscribers still drive ~73% of total revenue simply because they're 73% of the customer base.
- **Subscription upsell opportunity:** among customers with more than 5 previous purchases (clearly loyal), only ~28% are actually subscribed — a large pool of loyal, unsubscribed customers to target.
- **Clothing leads the category mix** at 44.5% of all orders, followed by Accessories (31.8%), Footwear (15.4%), and Outerwear (8.3%).
- **Revenue mirrors customer mix, not gender-based spending:** male customers make up 68% of the base and generate 68% of revenue, but average spend per customer is nearly identical across genders (~$59.53 male vs. ~$60.25 female).
- **Express shipping customers spend ~3.5% more per order** on average ($60.48) than Standard shipping customers ($58.46).
- **Top-rated products** (by average review rating) are Gloves, Sandals, Boots, Hat, and Handbag, all clustered between 3.78–3.86 out of 5.
